Rowing for Women Who Dare

Peregrine Rowing Club was founded to provide opportunities for women aged 40 and over to engage in competitive and recreational rowing. Operating from the historic Catz Boat house, we focus on both performance racing and the pure joy of being on the water.

Whether you're a seasoned rower or picking up an oar for the first time, you'll find a welcoming community that celebrates every stroke forward.

Our Achievements

From local bumps to international waters, the Peregrines have made their mark on the rowing world.

100-Mile Charity Row

Completed an incredible 100-mile relay row raising funds for Breast Cancer Now, demonstrating strength, endurance and heart.

Vogalonga Venice

Represented Cambridge at the prestigious 48th Vogalonga Regatta, rowing through the historic canals of Venice.

On the Cam

Regular competitors in the Cambridgeshire Rowing Association Town Bumps and local Cam races.

Beginners Welcome

Learn To Row

Never picked up an oar? No problem. Each year we run a dedicated Learn To Row programme designed specifically for complete beginners. Over several weeks, you'll master the fundamentals of rowing technique, build confidence on the water, and discover whether the Peregrine community is right for you.

Our experienced coaches will guide you through everything from your first stroke to rowing as part of a crew. Many of our current members started their rowing journey through this programme.
